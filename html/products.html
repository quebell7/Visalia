Yogurt

Vatset Yogurt - During the vatset method, pasteurized milk goes into a large vessel, or tank, and inoculation and incubation take place inside the vessel.

Cupset Yogurt - In the cupset process, milk is inoculated with cultures, filled directly into cups and incubated at 110* F until the desired acidity is reached. After pH is reached, the yogurt is placed into a cooler, containing large fans-to successfully distribute air flow.

Drinkable Yogurt - Drinkable yogurt is similar, in fashion, to traditional yogurt; the viscosity is thinner than traditional yogurt and oftentimes contains less starch than regular yogurt.

Kefir - Kefir is made with a yeast/bacterial fermentation starter and is often sour, due to the lactic acid bacteria formation.

Sour Cream

Sour Cream consists of the fermentation (or souring) of cream due to creating certain strains of lactic acid bacteria.
